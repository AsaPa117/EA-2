Reflection
 - Activity Description: The activity I've joined is called Hardware Hackathon. Our goal was to design a project that fits the theme, "seamless living". For the theme, our team and I chose to create a device that can detect and warn homeowners when a kitchen appliance is left on and unattended. There were two major layors that my partners and I were each responsible for. My specific tasks lie in the processing system, where the CPU runs and handles/creates instructions and readings to the microcontroller.
 - Technical Decisions: Although it'll be nice to design a device to handle every kitchen appliance, my partner and I knew that handling it would be too much for us. So we scope it down to an oven. There were two priorities we focused on: emphasizing our work on the hardware to signal flags via LEDs and using sensors to detect human presence and temperature. Due to limited hardware components, we had to adjust our plan and re-evaluate in some cases. I was the one who decided which sensors to use based on their availability and acknowledged some details about how they work. 
 - Contribution: I participated in the event with a team, and my sole responsibility focused on the PS aspect of the project. I was tasked with providing input for the peripheral device, assigning instructions, managing sensor readings through bit logic, and setting up the overall workflow to define the device behavior. I used Vitis and wrote the code in C.    
- Quality Assessment: This event has been a great experience. It allowed me to expand my ability to work with hardware components and taught me a lot about their purpose and how to connect to and communicate with them. If I were to redo the event, I would have researched and prepared for the equipment they provided, since a lot was unavailable and I didn’t know how to use much of it.
